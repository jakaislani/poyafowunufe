Meet Big Scarr Parents And Family: Where Are They From?

Big Scarr (2000-2022) is no more. Learn in detail about the parents and family members whom he has left behind.

Alexander Woods, known by his stage name Big Scarr, was a rapper signed to Gucci Mane’s 1017 Records.

The American rapper was best known for his track SoIcyBoyz, featuring Pooh Shiesty, Foogiano, and Tay Keith.

Scarr released his debut mixtape called Big Grim Reaper last year, which peaked at number 25 on the Billboard 200.

Unfortunately, he died aged 22 on December 22, 2022, from a drug overdose.

Big Scarr Parents And Family: Where Are They From? 

Alexander Woods was born and raised in the Magnolia community of South Memphis, Tennesse, United States.

His parents’ names are not mentioned anywhere. Born on April 7, 2000, he was only 22 at death. His birth sign was Aries.

Woods came from a big family with a total of eight siblings. At first, he lived with his grandmother until her demise when he was 13.

Fellow rapper Pooh Shiesty was Woods’ cousin. He featured Pooh in his track SoIcyBoyz.

All Famous Birthdays notes that his Father raised him in South Memphis. Likewise, Kazi Magazine states that he embraced his dad as his role model.

“My Father, my dawg. He cared for my siblings and me while still teaching me the game. He is a real G, and I will stand on that hill forever,” Big Scarr talked about his love for his Father.

The magazine described Big Scarr as the type of artist more artists should take after, noting that he embraced his past instead of running from it.

Like his Father, he also respected his label owner and mentor, Gucci Mane. “Gucci helped keep Pooh [my cousin] and me out of some Memphis sh*t, so I appreciate him being there,” Woods said.

“Without him, none of this would’ve happened. In our communities, we, as black men, need to guide our boys into men. One day I hope to do that,” he added.

Rapper Big Scarr Net Worth Before Death

Big Scarr had a net worth of $1.2 million before his death, per Freshers Live. He was already a millionaire at the young age of 22.

Scarr attained widespread fame and success through his solo career and as a member of the hip-hop collective and independent label Rich N Ruthless. 

Coming off a successful debut album release of Big Grim Reaper, he had viral videos like the SoIcyBoyz series and Make A Play.

With the success of his label mates Pooh Shiesty and Foogiano, Scarr was expected to be the next big thing until his untimely and unprecedented demise.

Big Scarr Had A Tumultuous Childhood

Big Scarr had been tested in the trenches of his hometown’s Magnolia district. However, it did not affect him; he came out victorious.

Before his death, Woods enjoyed life and had achieved considerable success and a fast-growing fan base at a young age.

The Memphis star made waves worldwide with his quality and high-selling trap music. Moreover, he accepted himself for who he was.

“I’m just myself; I’m a product of the environment I’m from,” Woods said. “From the times I got shot to being reckless in my youth, my music reflects that. I hope to inspire the youth to do good.”

When he was 20, he was shot in the hip. The bullet went to his spine, and he had to have his appendix removed and his right leg back into alignment.

Likewise, when Woods was 16, he met a car accident that threw him through the windshield of his friend’s car. He described the accident as the origin of his stage name, “Big Scarr.”

He started rapping with his friend Baby K. His single Make A Play got the attention of fellow rapper Gucci Mane, who later signed him to his record label.

Big Scarr exuded a rare level of openness and vulnerability, making listeners appreciate his music much more. He constantly referenced his scars and mistakes in his piece.

His music inspirations included Kodak Black, Boosie Badazz, and Rod Wave.
