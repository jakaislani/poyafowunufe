Lainey Wilson Weight Loss: How the Country Star Transformed Her Body

Lainey Wilson, an American country music singer-songwriter, started performing at a young age. She later moved to Nashville, Tennessee, to pursue her dream of becoming a country performer.

Recently, Lainey Wilson’s weight loss has caught the attention of fans. The singer and actress, who plays Summer Higgins on the hit show Yellowstone, has reportedly lost 70 pounds over several years by following a healthy diet and exercise regimen. In this article, we will explore how Lainey Wilson achieved her weight loss goals.

Lainey Wilson Weight Loss

Lainey decided to make 2020 her “year of health.” The Yellowstone star started eating more fruits and veggies while saying goodbye to sugar, dairy, and processed foods. She made smart choices, like snacking on raw veggies instead of unhealthy treats, and replaced sugary sodas with refreshing lemon water.

Her efforts paid off as she noticed positive changes. Lainey lost inches and achieved six-pack abs, making her feel confident in her clothes, especially when wearing crop tops. Alongside working with a personal trainer, she stayed active by hiking and swimming to keep fit.

Lainey Wilson Before and After Photos

Lainey Wilson has undergone a noticeable change, and pictures showing her before and after are making the rounds. Her fans are cheering for her new look, and she’s become an inspiration to many.

What Did Lainey Wilson Say About Losing Weight?

The singer of “Heart Like a Truck” talked about staying in shape by performing a lot on stage. In a video on Instagram on June 20, 2023, she said,

“If I have lost weight, it’s because I’m working hard, playing hour-and-a-half shows and running around every night of my life and to be honest, me losing weight or gaining weight ain’t got anything to do with my music.”

The singer talked about this after people wondered if she used diet supplements when she was named the 2022 CMA Female Vocalist of the Year. She said,

“By now I’m sure a lot of y’all have seen some ads about me losing weight, being hospitalized and then I started taking some weight loss gummies and blah, blah, blah, it saved my life. Well, surprise. It ain’t true.”
