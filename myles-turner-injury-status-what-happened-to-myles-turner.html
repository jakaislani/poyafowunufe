Who is Myles Turner?

Professional basketball player Myles Turner is well-known for his contributions to the National Basketball Association (NBA) Indiana Pacers. He was born in Bedford, Texas, on March 24, 1996. He weighs 250 pounds and has a remarkable height of 6 feet 11 inches.

Turner wears jersey number 33 and plays center/power forward for the Pacers. In the 2015 NBA Draft, he was drafted in the first round by the Indiana Pacers with the 11th overall pick, having played college basketball for the Texas Longhorns.

Myles Turner Early Life

Myles Turner was born in Bedford, Texas, to Anthony Davis and Mary Turner. This is where his basketball career started. He showed his talent at a young age, having taken up the sport at the age of six while attending Central Junior High School. In his career, Turner led his team to 28 victories despite only being a 6-foot-2-inch freshman.

He suffered a setback in his AAU play due to a broken ankle, but he recovered and became a standout player by his junior year, averaging an impressive 15.7 points, 12.87 rebounds, and 8.64 blocks per game.

Turner’s outstanding play carried over into his senior year, when he became a highly sought-after recruit and attracted national attention. In the end, he decided to play basketball for the University of Texas at the collegiate level.

Myles Turner Career

Myles Turner’s NBA career took off after the Indiana Pacers selected him in the 2015 NBA draft. He has been an important member of the team ever since, making major contributions in a number of seasons. Turner, who is well-known for his versatility and shot-blocking prowess, was named team captain prior to the 2017–2018 campaign.

In October 2018, he inked a $72 million, four-year contract extension with the Pacers. One of Turner’s most memorable performances was on October 22, 2021, when he scored a career-high 40 points against the Washington Wizards.
