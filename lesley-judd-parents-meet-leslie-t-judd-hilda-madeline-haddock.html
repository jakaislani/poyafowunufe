Lesley Judd Parents: Meet Leslie T. Judd &amp; Hilda Madeline Haddock

Lesley Judd is the daughter of Leslie T. Judd and Hilda Madeline Haddock. There isn’t much information about Lesley’s parents on the internet as she has kept her personal life away from the limelight.

Lesley Judd’s early exposure to the entertainment world came in 1959 when she appeared in the BBC Television adaptation of “Heidi.” Educated at the Arts Educational School in Tring Park, Hertfordshire, her journey into the limelight began as a child actress, starring in the Z-Cars episode “Person Unknown” in 1962.

The late 1960s saw Judd as part of the dance troupe “The Young Generation,” appearing on various television shows. However, a breach of contract led to her departure. Undeterred, she embarked on a new chapter, securing roles in films like “Half a Sixpence” (1967) and “And Now for Something Completely Different” (1971).

Lesley Judd entered the Blue Peter stage in 1972, joining a lineup that included Valerie Singleton, John Noakes, and Peter Purves.

Her partnership with Noakes and Purves endured until 1978, making it the show’s longest-running lineup. Despite contractual uncertainties and personal challenges, including a storm surrounding her first marriage to Derek Fowlds, Judd remained steadfast on Blue Peter until 1979.
