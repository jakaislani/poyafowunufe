What is a cripple stud in construction?

Jul 15, 2024 Answer A cripple stud is a structural member that is situated above or below a door or window. An injured rafter is one that extends from the hip to the valley of a roof or is cut short to accommodate an aperture such as a chimney or skylight, among other things. Neither cripple studs nor cripple rafters are built of a different stock than normal studs or rafters; they are simply cut to a shorter length.
